Composting

Start Composting at Home

Published on March 15, 2024

Composting is a natural process that transforms kitchen and garden waste into valuable fertilizer. This guide will help you start your own composting system at home.

Benefits of Composting

  • Reduces household waste by up to 30%
  • Creates nutrient-rich soil for your garden
  • Helps reduce greenhouse gas emissions
  • Saves money on fertilizers and waste collection

What You Can Compost

Green Materials (Nitrogen-Rich)

  • Fruit and vegetable scraps
  • Coffee grounds and tea bags
  • Fresh grass clippings
  • Plant trimmings

Brown Materials (Carbon-Rich)

  • Dry leaves
  • Straw and hay
  • Paper and cardboard
  • Wood chips

Getting Started

  1. Choose a suitable location in your yard
  2. Select your composting method (bin, pile, or tumbler)
  3. Layer green and brown materials
  4. Keep the pile moist but not wet
  5. Turn the pile regularly

Troubleshooting Common Issues

If your compost pile smells bad or isn't decomposing properly, check the moisture level and balance of materials. A healthy compost pile should be slightly damp and have a good mix of green and brown materials.
